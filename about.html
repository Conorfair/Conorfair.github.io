Updated CV

I am originally from Minnesota where I grew up exploring many lakes and woods. Summers were spent at Many Point Scout Camp where I developed my passion for teaching and nature/the outdoors. I earned a Bachelor of Arts degree with a major in Biology and minors in Chemistry and Spanish from Simpson College in Indianola Iowa. While studying at Simpson College, I completed various research projects in plant and insect ecology with my advisor Dr. Clint Meyer. I also applied and was selected to participate in an NSF REU (Research Experience for Undergraduates) position in the Department of Entomology at Texas A&M University. I worked with Dr. Gregory Sword and Dr. Kevin Heinz studying endophytic entomopathogenic fungi and their inhibition of leafminer fly larvae development within ornamental plants.

As a Master's student in the Department of Entomology at the University of Georgia under the advisement of Dr. Kris Braman, my thesis research investigated the effects of inter-cropping floral resources to attract beneficial insects to help control the squash bug (Anasa tristis) and the damage/yield loss in small-scale organic squash produciton. We also characterized the predation/feeding behavior of the big-eyed bug (Geocoris punctipes) on A. tristis eggs and early nymphs.

I also worked with Dr. Joe McHugh in the Department of Entomology at the University of Georgia and Dr. Mike Ulyshen at the Southeastern research Station in the USDA Forest Service for my PhD. My dissertation research focused on the landscape-scale effects of prescribed fires on native pollinator communities in the Southeastern United States. In collobration with a then-postdoctoral research associate, I helped develop an on-line synchronous course for major and non-major undergraduate students. This was such a formative experience to build a course from scratch, organize active learning activities for on-line learning environment, and adapt to unique teaching conditions during the COVID-19 pandemic.

Since completing my PhD., I am lucky enough to stay in Athens, GA and work with Dr. Kris Braman again! I am combining my applied agricultural research from my Master's degree and the pollinator conservation research from my PhD. I have enjoyed advising undergraduate students with indepentdent research projects and in collaboration with my own research. It is very rewarding to return the favor given to me as an undergraduate researcher! I am also teaching a graduate level course in quantitative analysis (another passion of mine).
