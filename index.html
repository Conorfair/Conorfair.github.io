IPM Research in Small-Scale Organic Squash Production

My thesis research studied the cultural control methods of planting floral resources to control populations of the squash bug Anasa tristis. I monitored beneficial insect populations throughout this study to determine if the floral resources provided benefit through conservation biological control.

    Insect Ecologist

    Studying basic and applied questions related to management decisions that affect pest insect populations and native pollinator communities

    Insect pests comprise one to three percent of known insect species, but they account for extensive economic losses and other related effects on everyday human life. My research seeks to understand how to use Integrated Pest Management (IPM) strategies to help mitigate these losses and prevent populations of various insects from reaching harmful levels. This research serves the needs of citizens, industry professionals, and other interested parties.

    Insect pollinators, of which bees are considered to be the most consequential broadly, provide an incredibly important ecosystem service. Pollination by bees and other insects is responsible for approximately 35% of food production in the United States. Given the increasing evidence for the decline of pollinator communities worldwide as caused by agricultural intensification among other factors, we are investigating the impacts of human distrubances and other land management methods on native pollinator communities.

    I am broadly interested in the following:

    • Measuring the impact of various management decisions on pest insect populations and native pollinator community interactions
    • Understanding the drivers of human behavioral psychology that leads to the adoption of behaviors demonstrated to support pollinator conservation
    • Developing outreach and educational programs to help educate and enable people to help manage pest insects and protect and support pollinators

    I'm open to new questions and ideas! If you are interested in supporting pollinators, don't hesitate to contact me.
